Tiering follows CBRE's Global Data Center Trends classification (primary = the seven largest US wholesale markets; secondary = mature smaller markets; emerging = recently-active build-out markets). Market-level MW figures are illustrative seed values rounded to published industry ranges; the Facilities layer seeds 30+ named buildings with operator, developer, and funding source. Refresh via POST /api/data-centers/refresh. Spec ratio = under-construction MW × (1 − pre-leased %) ÷ built MW; pipeline ratio = (UC + planned) ÷ built; concentration = market share of national built MW. Funding-source taxonomy: hyperscaler self-fund, public REIT, infra fund / PE, sovereign/strategic JV (e.g. Stargate), specialty/venture, public specialty.

Stranded-asset risk (0–100) = tenant concentration (0–30) + tenant credit quality (0–20) + speculative build component (0–15) + funding-source resilience (0–10) + geographic correlation (0–10) + tenant balance-sheet stretch (committed AI capex vs. tenant FCF, 0–15). At-risk MW applies a default-probability matrix on (tenant tier × status × scenario). The scenario selector at the top of the page swaps in three calibrations: Mild (capex normalizes), Moderate (demand neutralizes), Severe (AI demand crash). Calibration is illustrative — substitute house probabilities by editing _SCENARIOS and TENANT_FCF_HEADROOM in backend/data_centers/service.py.
