US Macro Model

A reduced-form econometric model of the US economy in the FRB/US lineage. Eleven estimated behavioral equations spanning spending, prices, labor, and financial blocks, solved jointly every quarter. Produces baseline forecasts, bootstrap fan charts, shock-scenario impulse responses, and out-of-sample backtests against actual FRED data — not a scorecard, not a weighted index, a real macro model.

Methodology. Engle-Granger two-step error correction per equation (long-run cointegrating relation on levels, short-run dynamics on differences with AIC lag selection). System solved jointly each quarter via damped Gauss-Seidel. Confidence bands come from residual bootstrapping (resample fitted-sample residuals). Shock scenarios rewrite the exogenous path; IRFs are the baseline-minus-shocked difference for every endogenous variable. Not Bayesian, no fiscal block, adaptive expectations only — this is a v1. See backend/macro_model/README.md for known limitations.
